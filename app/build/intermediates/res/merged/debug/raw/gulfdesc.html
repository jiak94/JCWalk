The Gulf War was instigated on August 2, 1990 when Iraqi forces, under Saddam Hussein, invaded oil rich Kuwait, a small nation in the Middle East that is bordered by the Persian Gulf. On that same day the U. N. passed a resolution denouncing the invasion and only four days following that condemnation, on August 6, imposed sanctions on Iraq. On August 7, President George H. W. Bush commanded implementation of Operation Desert Shield, the designation for the build-up of forces in Saudi Arabia and other Gulf nations. Desert Shield would last thru January 15, 1991. And then, on January 16, Desert Shield would be named Operation Desert Storm.

Despite months of U. N. sanctions and the massing of troops and weapons in Saudi Arabia during Operation Desert Shield, Saddam could not be persuaded to withdraw from Kuwait and to abandon his intent to confiscate the oil resources of that small nation. And, since August 8, when he had defiantly annexed that small country, nothing could persuade him to withdraw his army of occupation. The presumed reason for the takeover was to pay the huge debt that Iraq had acquired during its disastrous eight year war with Iran, its powerful neighbor to the east that Saddam had also invaded. Thus, there were geopolitical, oil revenue and territorial considerations that prompted Saddam to defy the world order and to hold on tenaciously in Kuwait.

Events continued to unfold and on August 25, the U. N. passed a resolution to allow enforcement of its embargo by military means if necessary. Iraq refused to budge and on November 29, 1990, the U. N. authorized the use of force to remove the Iraqi forces if they were not withdrawn by January 15, 1991.

With no Iraqi reaction to the November ultimatum for Saddam to withdraw from Kuwait, it was only two days following the set deadline when, on the morning of January 17th, the fury of Operation Desert Storm was unleashed against the war mongering Saddam Hussein. And, for the next five weeks, Iraqi targets were pounded by naval and air bombardments against targets in Iraq and Kuwait.

Then, on February 24th, the U. N. coalition, under the command of U. S. General Norman Schwarzkopf, launched the ground invasion to drive the Iraqi regiments from Kuwait. Saddam’s army was no match for the predominantly American detachments and within one hundred hours following the ground offensive, Baghdad radio suddenly announced that Iraq would comply with the U. N. resolution to withdraw.

However, before Saddam’s capitulation, air raid sirens had wailed throughout Israel and thousands of Israelis were forced to flee to underground covers to escape the incoming Scud missiles. The first of those missiles was intercepted and destroyed over Dhahran, Saudi Arabia by an American Patriot missile. However one such Scud was fired at an American barracks in Dhahran. That lone Scud missile fulfilled its mission and some 26 American soldiers lay dead following that attack.

Casualties

Even though the Gulf War lasted for only eight and a half months from August 2, 1990 until it was declared over on April 11, 1991, and U. S. troops had participated in actual combat for only six weeks during Operation Desert Storm, some 293 American service members were returned home in coffins. Some 148 had been killed in action and another 145 deaths were caused by non-hostile actions. And, an addition 776 American troops were wounded. It is also estimated that Iraq lost up to 35,000 men killed with more than 75,000 wounded.

Conclusion

The U. N. force consisted of some 670,000 troops from twenty eight countries and the

U. S. supplied more than 425,000 of that number. However, despite the size of that U. N. Force, the retreating Iraqi’s did not leave quietly. During their forced retreat from Kuwait, they created a huge oil slick in the Persian Gulf and set fire to over 700 oil wells in that small country, actions that did immense harm to the earths’ environment.

The estimated cost of the Gulf War amounted to some $61 billion with Saudi Arabia, Kuwait and other Gulf States covering some $36 billion of that total liability.
